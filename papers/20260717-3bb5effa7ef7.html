Silicon-photonic optomechanical magnetometer

期刊
Optica

英文摘要

Optomechanical sensors enable exquisitely sensitive force measurements, with emerging applications across quantum technologies, standards, fundamental science, and engineering. Magnetometry is among the most promising applications, where chip-scale optomechanical sensors offer high sensitivity without the cryogenics or magnetic shielding required by competing technologies. However, a lack of compatibility with integrated photonics and electronics has posed a major barrier. Here, we introduce silicon-on-insulator optomechanical magnetometers to address this barrier. A new post-release lithography process enables high-quality metallization of released mechanical structures, overcoming the incompatibility between silicon-on-insulator fabrication and functional magnetic films. This allows us to employ photonic-crystal cavities that enhance motion-to-optical signal transduction by over an order of magnitude. The resulting devices achieve magnetic field sensitivity of 800pT/Hz, three orders of magnitude beyond previous waveguide-integrated designs. The advance report we provide, leads to a path toward high-performance, room temperature, and chip-integrated magnetometers for applications ranging from biomedical imaging and navigation to resource exploration.
